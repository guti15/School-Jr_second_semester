 On almi&d;in drichtines name. 
 Ic Eadward king ky&d;e alle manne &d;e red &d;at ic mid alle mine redgiuen red habbe for mine soule &t;erfe and for alle mine &t;edchipes sindfulnesse. 
 &D;at is &d;at ic an eke fredames &d;an halegen kinge seint Eadmunde so for&d; so he fermest hauede into &d;ere stowe &d;e he onrestet. 
 An so wille him &d;at se fredom stonde on his welde  &d;at so fele si&d;e so men gilde&d; hire gilde to heregilde  o&d;er to schipgeld  gilde &d;e tunschipe so o&d;ere men don to &d;e abbotes nede and &d;ere moneke &d;e &d;er binine schulen for us seruen  and &d;e abboteriche &d;at Cnut king &d;er ches  and si&d;en Hardecnut king mine bro&d;er also it sta&d;elfaste. 
 And ic su&d;en eac swilke mid &d;ere schirebiscopes rede  and mid mine wedde  and alle mine biscope  and alle mine wytene it fultremede &d;at ic nefre ne schulle ani man wenden to o&d;ere hodes manne  buten he wolde ben aschired from Godes manne and alle hise halegen. 
 And ic an &d;an halegen kinge &d;at land at Mildenhale mid mete and mid manne and mid sokne so it me on hande stod  and &d;e halfnigende hundredes sokne into &d;inghowe. 
 And ic han hem al here tune sokene of hale here londe &d;e he nu habbe&d; o&d;er giet bigiten schullen on Godes este and on mines an alle folkes. 
 God how healde.
