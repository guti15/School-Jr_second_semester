 On min Drihtines name ic Eadwine kithe ihu &t;at ic an &t;e &t;inge &t;e me God almithin haue lent on &t;ise liue. 
 &T;at is &t;at ic an &t;at lond at Eskeresthorp into seint Eadmund buten ten acres ic giue &t;er into &t;ere kirke. 
 And Lefric habbe &t;o &t;re acres &t;e he on sit. 
 And ic an &t;at lond at Lithle Meddeltone into sancte Benedicte &amp; ten acres into &t;e kirke. 
 And ic an &t;at lond at Beorh into sancte Etheldri&d;e bi suthen kinges strete buten &t;at northere hage at Appelsco. 
 And half &t;e turfgret ligge into Apetune and &t;erto weye to rode brod. 
 And ten akres into Beorh kirke be suthen strete and ten acres into Apetune kirke bi northen strete &amp; four acres into Huluestone kirke and four acres into Blitleford kirke. 
 And X acres into Sparham kirke &amp; alle mine men fre eywer aftir mine tyme. 
 &T;is is &t;at frescet &t;e Eadwine haue&d; Crist vnnen and seynte Marie &amp; alle Cristes halegen his sowle to alisidnesse and his sinne tor forgiuenesse. 
 And &t;is is &t;e forward &t;e Wlfric and Eadwine &t;a tueye brethere wrouhten hem bituen ymbe &t;a to land at Thorp and at Middeltone. 
 &T;at is wether here so lenger libbe habbe bothe &t;e lond and after here bo&t;ere day go &t;at lond at Middiltone into sancte Benedicte for here bo&t;ere soule and &t;at lond at Thorpe after here bo&t;ere day fange Ketel &t;erto to swilke forwarde so &t;er wrouht is. 
 &T;at is &t;at Ketel leste alke iher to pund into seynt Eadmunde  &t;at is &t;as londes gouel  &amp; for here ey&t;eres soule ilke day ane messe. 
 And after Keteles day go &t;at lond into seynt Eadmunde buten alken gentale and at Metheltone into &t;ere kirke &t;e &t;urwerd ahte and &t;at lond &t;e Eadwine Ecfer&t;es sune ahte fre into &t;e kirke and of &t;at lond at Thorp VIII acres into Aescewelle kirke and of &t;at lond at Wreningham VIII acres into &t;e elde kirke and to acres into Fundenhale kirke and to into Neolondes kirke. 
 &T;ise write sinden &t;re  on is binnen at sancte Eadmunde  an other at sancte Benedicte on Holm &amp; te &t;ridde haued Eadwine himself.
